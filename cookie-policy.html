Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 2026  ·  CapeLoad Logistics (Pty) Ltd, Cape Town, South Africa

This policy explains how CapeLoad uses cookies and similar browser storage technologies, and how you can manage your preferences at any time.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by a website. They allow the site to remember information about your visit — such as whether you are logged in — and help us improve your experience. We also use localStorage, a similar browser technology that stores data locally on your device without expiry unless cleared.

2. How We Use Cookies

We group cookies into three categories. You can manage your preferences for the optional categories at any time using the button at the bottom of this page.

Necessary Always Active

These cookies are essential for the website to function. They enable core features such as logging in, maintaining your session, and keeping the site secure. Without these cookies the site cannot operate, so they cannot be disabled.

Name Purpose Duration Provider

Analytics Optional

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with the site — which pages are visited most, where errors occur, and how quickly pages load. All data is aggregated and anonymised; we cannot identify you from analytics data alone.

We do not currently run an active analytics tool. When one is introduced, we will update this table and seek your consent again if needed.

Marketing cookies are used to personalise content, remember your newsletter subscription preferences, and measure the effectiveness of our promotional communications. We do not sell your data to advertisers.

3. Managing Your Preferences

You can review and change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the button below or the cookie icon that appears at the bottom-left of every page.

4. Browser Controls

You can also control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Note that blocking necessary cookies will prevent you from logging in or using core features of the site. For guidance:

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Settings → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

5. Do Not Track

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. We currently do not alter the site's behaviour based on DNT signals, but we will update this policy if our approach changes.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Material changes that affect optional cookie categories will trigger a new consent banner.
